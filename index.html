Why Ocho Rios?

Las Choreras, 'The Waterfalls'

Encompassing the garden parish of St. Ann, the resort area of Ocho Rios is the nature lover’s escape. Fabled to be watered by eight rivers (as its name suggests) Ocho Rios provides a lush landscape of caves, gardens, rivers and white sand beaches. The name Ocho Rios actually derives from Las Choreras, meaning "the waterfalls" and may be a reflection of the many waterfalls in the area. From the Fern Gully located in the remains of an old river bed to the tiered waterfalls of Dunn’s River, arguably, Jamaica’s most popular attraction. Ocho Rios draws thousands of visitors back to arguably the island’s most romantic, picturesque resort area.

Ocho Rios Bay Beach is a premier destination renowned for its soft white sands and crystal-clear waters.

Margaritaville

Indulge in signature dishes like Jimmy Buffett's Cheeseburger in Paradise, Volcano Nachos, and then wash it all down with the Perfect Margarita. From island-inspired entrees to hand-crafted cocktails, every bite and sip brings a taste of paradise to the heart of Manhattan.

Turtle Beach Rd, Ocho Rios, Jamaica

What I Like:

A large venue with plenty of outside space and activities to enjoy. The menu is out of this world, with tasty burgers, nachos, fresh seafood, not to mention the delicious signature cocktails that you can purchase in a reusable Margaritaville cup.

Street Eats Tour

Enjoy a mouth-watering tour around the town of Ocho Rios, sampling local specialties and Jamaican street food. As you walk through the downtown area, your guide will take you off the typical touristy streets and give you insights about the town’s food, architecture and history.

Address:

Check Tour Operator's Guide

What I Like:

In my opinion, you cannot say you have vacationed in Jamaica without sampling some traditional street food. If you embark on a guided street food tour, you will visit approximately 5 to 7 authentic street vendors and local food shops where you will taste a variety of local foods and delicacies. Finally, you’ll visit a local bar where you can sip on Jamaican rum or sodas.
